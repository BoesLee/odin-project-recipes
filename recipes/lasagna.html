Some Regarded Belgian's
Lasagna

Because it ain't no traditional Lasagna.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna Pasta Sheets

    • Enough Italian Soft Wheat Flour
    • Some "Large" Eggs
      Note: 1 Egg per 100 Gram ( 0.03 Ounce ) of Flour
    • A pinch of salt

  • Bolognese Sauce

    • Some Olive Oil
    • Maybe Four Garlic Cloves
    • Probably Three Bay Leaves
    • Around Two Celery Stalks
    • Possibly One Eggplant
    • Surely a Bunch of Fresh Tomatoes
    • About One Kilogram of Minced Meat
      Note: 50% Beef - 50% Pork is Recommended
    • Couple of Habanero Peppers
      Note: Cheese Sauce Will Lower the Heat but One Should Try Not to Murder Your / Someones / Everyones Asshole
    • Fresh Basil, Salt, Pepper, Herbes de Provence, dried Jalapeño Flakes.
    • Some Sun-Dried Tomatoes
      Note: Optional, but encouraged

  • Mornay Sauce aka the Sauce for Macaroni and Cheese

    • Plenty of Butter
    • An Equal Amount of All Purpose Flour
    • Big Bottle of Milk
    • One Average Onion, Peeled
    • Pepper, Salt, Fresh Nutmeg and Cayenne
    • Some Equal Amounts of Grated:
      • Parmigiano Reggiano
      • Emmental
      • Gruyère
      • Gouda

Instructions

  • Lasagna Pasta Sheets

    1. Mound Flour on large Board, make Well and add Pinch of Salt.
    2. Slowly and Steadily Mix Eggs with Inside Flour until "No Longer Runny" has been achieved
    3. Bring Outside Flour In, Forming Large Mass on Board
    4. Knead Like itsa Bread until Dough achieved Smooth, Elastic, and just slightly Tacky Status.
      Note: Dust Dough with more Flour if too damn Sticky, but too much Flour makes Tough Pasta
    5. Roll Dough in Ball and Wrap in Cling film
    6. 30-ish Minutes Downtime
    7. Take ⅙th Dough, Re-wrap Rest to Avoid Dry Out.
    8. Roll until Flat enough
      Note: to Pass through Pasta Machine or Thin enough to Almost See Fingers.
    9. Cut into desired size
    10. Let Dry
      Note: on Rack or on Lightly Floured Fine Tea Towel placed on a Wire Mesh Oven Tray (or similar) on Top of some Oven Dish ( for Air Circulation )
    11. Repeat from Step 7 until End of Dough
    12. Store for Later
      Note: (Can be Stored, when Completely Dry and Stiff, for up to 1 week )

  • Bolognese Sauce

    1. Dice Vegetables
      Note: Tomatoes are Berries!
    2. Crush Garlic Cloves
    3. Chop Up Peppers
    4. Throw Olive Oil and/or Butter in Pot/Pan, add Garlic and Peppers
      • If ( One Cares about Different Levels of Heat )
        1. Grab and Add Diced Vegetables, Fry until Soft and
        2. Fry Minced Meat in Separate Pan/Pot until Beginnings of Brown appear, then add ( to ) the Vegetables.
      • If ( One Cares about the Dishes )
        1. Grab and Add Diced Vegetables, Fry until Soft
        2. Add Meat to Pan/Pot until Beginnings of Brown appears
    6. Add Tomatoes and Bay Leaves
    7. Lower Heat, Let Simmer ( Partially Covered ), Stirring Occasionally
      Note: If using Fresh or Uncooked Pasta the Sauce needs a Little Liquidy
    8. 2-ish Hours "Downtime"
    9. Add Salt, Pepper, Herbes de Provence, dried Jalapeño Flakes to Taste
    10. Store Away for a Day
      Note: Optional, but encouraged

  • Mornay Cheese Sauce

    1. Melt Butter over Low Heat
    2. Mix with sifted Flour until Paste ( aka Roux )
      Note: Should smell a bit like Cookies and look Golden-ish
    3. Slowly Add Milk while Whisking Away
      Note: Lumps mean you fucked up.
    4. Add Onion, Let Simmer and Stir Occasionally
    5. 10-ish Minutes "Downtime"
    6. Take Pot of Fire and Remove Onion
    7. Slowly Add Small Portions of All the Cheeses
      Note: Do not use all the Cheese! One Needs enough to Top Off the Lasagna.
    8. Add Salt, Pepper, Fresh Nutmeg and Cayenne to Taste.

  • The Actual Lasagna

    1. Dice up Sun-Dried Tomatoes
    2. Cook the Pasta, in Boiling Salted Water
      Note: Add a bit of Olive Oil to the Water so they don't stick together...or cook them one at a time
    3. Butter Oven Dish Up
    4. Spread Layer Spaghetti Sauce Over it, Add Splash of Cheese Sauce and Sprinkle with Sun-Dried Tomatoes
    5. Add Layer of Pasta
    6. Spread Layer of Cheese Sauce, Add Splash of Spaghetti Sauce
    7. Add Layer of Pasta
    8. Repeat Steps 4 to 7 until Almost at Top of Oven Dish
      Note: Last Layer HAS to be Cheese Sauce! Plan accordingly!
    9. Cover with grated Cheese and then some Butter Flakes
      Note: To somewhat Avoid untimely Burning of Top Layer
    10. Put in Oven at 170-180 °c ( 338-356°F )
      Note: Test the Pasta with appropriate utencil
    11. 30-ish Minutes Downtime
    12. Fancy it Up with Some Basil
    13. Let Sit for 5-10 Minutes before Serving
      Last Note: Best done with Favorite Drink
