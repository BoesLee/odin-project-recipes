Some Regarded Belgian's
Spicy Lasagna

Because it's no traditional Lasagna


Ingredients

  • Lasagna Pasta Sheets

    • Enough Italian Soft Wheat Flour
    • Some "Large" Eggs
      NOTE: 1 Egg per 100 Gram ( 0.03 Ounce ) of Flour
    • A pinch of salt

  • Spicy Spaghetti Sauce

    • Some Olive Oil
    • Maybe Four Garlic Cloves
    • Probably Three Bay Leaves
    • Around Two Celery Stalks
    • Possibly One Eggplant
    • Surely a Bunch of Fresh Tomatoes
    • About One Kilogram of Minced Meat
      NOTE: 50% Beef - 50% Pork is Recommended
    • Couple of Habanero Peppers
      NOTE: Cheese Sauce Will Lower the Heat but One Should Try Not to Murder Your / Someones / Everyones Asshole
    • Fresh Basil, Salt, Pepper, Herbes de Provence, dried Jalapeño Flakes.
    • Some Sun-Dried Tomatoes
      NOTE: Optional, but encouraged

  • Mornay Sauce aka the Sauce for Macaroni and Cheese

    • Plenty of Butter
    • An Equal Amount of All Purpose Flour
    • Big Bottle of Milk
    • One Average Onion, Peeled
    • Pepper, Salt, Fresh Nutmeg and Cayenne
    • Some Equal Amounts of Grated:
      • Parmigiano Reggiano
      • Emmental
      • Gruyère
      • Gouda

Instructions

  • Lasagna Pasta Sheets

    1. Mound Flour on large Board, make Well and add Pinch of Salt.
    2. Slowly and Steadily Mix Eggs with Inside Flour until "No Longer Runny" has been achieved.
    3. Bring Outside Flour in, forming Large Mass on Board
    4. Knead like itsa Bread ( pushing down with Heel of Hand ) until Dough achieved Smooth, Elastic, and just slightly Tacky Status.
      NOTE: Dust Dough with more Flour if too damn Sticky, but too much Flour makes Tough Pasta
    5. Roll Dough in Ball and Wrap in Cling film.
    6. Take 30 minute Break

    1. Take ⅙ Dough, Re-wrap Rest to Avoid Dry Out.
    2. Roll until Flat enough to Pass through Pasta Machine or Thin enough to Almost See Fingers.
    3. Cut into desired size
    4. Let dry on rack or on a Lightly Floured Fine Tea Towel placed on a Wire Mesh Oven Tray (or similar) on Top of some Oven Dish ( for Air Circulation )
    5. Repeat from Step 7 until End of Dough
    6. Store till Use
      NOTE: (Can be Stored, when Completely Dry and Stiff, for up to 1 week )

  • Spicy Spaghetti Sauce

    1. Dice Vegetables to Small Dices
      NOTE: Tomatoes are Berries!
    2. Crush Garlic Cloves with flat Side of Knife
    3. Minorly Chop Up Peppers
    4. Throw Olive Oil and/or Butter in Pot/Pan, add Garlic and Peppers

      • If ( One Cares about Different Levels of Heat )
        1. Grab and Add Diced Vegetables, Fry until Soft and
        2. Fry Minced Meat in Separate Pan/Pot until Beginnings of Brown appear, then add ( to ) the Vegetables.

      • If ( One Cares about the Dishes )
        1. Grab and Add Diced Vegetables, Fry until Soft
        2. Add Meat to Pan/Pot until Beginnings of Brown appear

    2. Add Tomatoes and Bay Leaves
    3. Lower Heat, Let Simmer ( Partially Covered for 2+ hours, or until Sufficiently Reduced ), Stirring Occasionally.
      NOTE: If using Fresh or Uncooked Pasta the Sauce needs to be a Little Liquidy.
    4. Add Salt, Pepper, Herbes de Provence, dried Jalapeño Flakes to taste.
    5. Store it Away for a Day
      NOTE: Optional, but encouraged

  • Mornay Cheese Sauce

    1. Melt Butter over Low Heat
    2. Mix with sifted Flour until Paste ( aka Roux )
      NOTE: Should smell a bit like Cookies and look Golden-ish
    3. Slowly Add Milk while Whisking Away,
      NOTE: Avoid making it Runny by Adding Milk to Fast due to Tired Arm/Bored Brain
      Second NOTE: Lumps mean you fucked up.
    4. Add Onion, let simmer for ~ 10-ish minutes

    1. Take Pot of Fire, Remove Onion
    2. Slowly Add Small Portions of All the Cheeses, Keep Stirring Until Melted
      NOTE: Do not use all the Cheese! One Needs it to, at least, Top Off the Lasagna.
    3. Add Salt, Pepper, Fresh Nutmeg and Cayenne to taste.
    4. Store it Away for a Day
      NOTE: Optional, if made on the same day as the spaghetti sauce

  • The Actual Lasagna

    1. Dice up Sun-Dried Tomatoes
    2. Cook the pasta, in boiling salted water.
      NOTE: Add a bit of Olive Oil to the Water so they don't stick together...or cook them one at a time
    3. Butter Oven Dish Up

    1. Spread Layer Spaghetti Sauce Over it, Add Splash of Cheese Sauce and Sprinkle with Sun-Dried Tomatoes
    2. Add Layer of Pasta
    3. Spread Layer of Cheese Sauce, Add Splash of Spaghetti Sauce
    4. Add Layer of Pasta
    5. Repeat Steps 4 to 7 until Almost at Top of Oven Dish
      NOTE: LAST LAYER HAS TO BE THE CHEESE SAUCE! Plan accordingly!

    1. Cover with grated Cheese and then some Butter Flakes
      NOTE: To somewhat Avoid untimely Burning of Top Layer
      Another NOTE: If it does Start to Burn too hard too damn soon, Cover with Aluminium Foil
    2. Put in preheated Oven at 170-180 °c ( 338-356°F ) Until surface is Golden and Pasta in Cooked
      NOTE: Check with Fork or other Tool fit for Job, should take about 30-ish minutes
    3. Fancy it Up with Some Basil
    4. Let Sit for 5-10 minutes before serving.
      Last NOTE: Best done with Favorite Drink
