Some Regarded Belgian's
Vol-au-vent

Because it's a dish, not some silly appetizer.

Ingredients

  • Chicken Broth

    • Plenty of Chicken
      Note: Leftover Chicken Cuttings/Parts can be found at the local friendly Catering/Restaurant/Butcher, often by working there/asking nicely.
    • Some Carrots, Celeries and Onion.
    • Parsley, Thyme, Bay Leaf, Garlic and a Chili

  • The "Velouté"

    • An Amount of Minced Meat
      Note: 50% Beef - 50% Pork is Recommended
    • A Bowl of Breadcrumbs
      Note: Mix up old, dried out bread in some blender-thing
    • Plenty of Mushrooms
    • Some Egg Yolks
    • Some Star Anise
    • Half a Lemon
    • Plenty of Butter
    • An Equal Amount of All Purpose Flour
    • Some Salt, Pepper, Fresh Nutmeg and Cayenne
    • A Clove or Two of Garlic
    • Potatoes

Instructions

  • Chicken Broth

    1. Barely Cut Veggies
    2. Throw Chicken Stuff and the Veggies In the Pot with the Parsley, Thyme, Bay Leaf, Garlic and a Chili
      Note: Leave some Parsley for Later
    3. Fill up with Water
      Note: Cover Everything
    4. Let it Boil
    5. Lower Heat, Let Simmer ( Partially Covered ), Skimming Top Occasionally.
    6. 2-ish Hours "Downtime"
    7. Remove Chicken from Pot, Let it Cool
    8. Leave Broth Simmering until Needed
    9. Remove Meat from Cool Chicken

  • The "Velouté" Vol-au-vent

    1. Mix Breadcrumbs, Egg Yolks and Minced Meat.
      Note: Add Pepper, Salt and whatnot to the Mix
    2. Roll the Meat-Mix into Small, equal-ish sized Meatballs
      Note: Small means about a diameter of about ~ 1,5 cm ( thats ~ 0,6 inch )
    3. Bring Broth back to a Boil
    4. Grab Suitable Colander, Throw in Meatballs and Put in Broth
    5. Remove when Cooked
    6. Cut Mushrooms in Quarters
    7. Melt Butter in a pan, Add Mushrooms, then Garlic, Some Pepper and Salt to Taste
    8. Put Aside when Ready
    9. Melt Butter over Low Heat
    10. Mix with Sifted Flour until Paste ( aka Roux )
      Note: Should smell a bit like Cookies and look Golden-ish
    11. Slowly Add Broth ( trough a Colander ) while Whisking Away
      Note: Lumps mean you Fucked Up
    12. Add Mushrooms, Chicken Meat Pieces and Meatballs
    13. Add Star Anise
      Note: Usually, It's Supposed to Come Back Out before Serving, Some Tool to Retrieve it is Recommended
    14. Add Salt, Pepper, Fresh Nutmeg, some Cayenne and a drop of Lemon Juice to taste
      Note: It's not supposed to be spicy, but a bit of "warmth" is nice
    15. Laaadidaaa-It-Up with Some Shredded Parsley
    16. Serve with Some Form of Potatoes
